Economic Perspectives of Bone Marrow Registries

Joint with Fumio Ohtake (Osaka University), Kazuhiro Yoshiuchi (The University of Tokyo), Yukariko Hida (The University of Tokyo), Saiko Kurosawa (Ina Central Hospital), and Takahiro Fukuda (National Cancer Center Hospital)

When Recipients having blood diseases cannot (e.g. leukimia) find out related donors, they need a transplant of blood cells provided by donors who joined in Japanese Marrow Donor Program (JMDP). To acheive a transplant in a short time, JMDP should increase not only the size of donor pool but also the quality of donor pool. This project aims to reveal characteristics of providers from perspectives of economics, especially behavioral economics, using the survey data that JMDP carried out.

1. Behavioral Characteristics of Bone Marrow Registrants and Stem Cell Donors

“Behavioral Characteristics of Bone Marrow Registrants and Stem Cell Donors” (with Fumio Ohtake, Rena Shigeoka, Kazuhiro Yoshiuchi, Yukariko Hida, Saiko Kurosawa, Takahiro Fukuda), Journal of Behavioral Economics and Finance, 13, 32-52, 2020. [Japanese] [View full text]

Summary

This paper examines whether there are differences in behavioral characteristics between bone marrow bank registrants, stem cell donors, and the general population using a questionnaire survey of bone marrow bank registrants who have received notification of matching with a transplant patient, and a questionnaire survey of the general population at Osaka University. The main results are as follows.

  1. Registered bone marrow bankers and stem cell donors are more altruistic, have lower time discount rates, and have a higher risk tolerance than the general population.
  2. The probability of stem cell donation is higher among regular blood donors and those who have indicated their intention to donate their organs.
  3. The probability of stem cell donation is higher in an environment where paid donor leave and paid holidays are readily available.
  4. Individuals with high conformity are more likely to register with a bone marrow bank, but are more likely not to donate when asked to do so.
  5. Although the time discount rates and present bias of registrants and donors are lower than in the general population, those with higher time discount rates, including present bias, have a higher probability of donating stem cells.

2. Social Image and Bone Marrow Registries

Summary

This paper analyzes how social image associated with registration, a subjective probability of being asked, and time preference affects the quality of donor pool. The social image gives an incentive to join in the donor pool to those who do not want to provide their own stem cells. Moreover, since provision is a probabilistic and future event, utility coming from provision (or non-provision) is discounted by the subjective probability and a time discount factor. These two factors also give an incentive of participation to less altruistic persons. Thus, we expect that stimulation of social image and decreases of the subjective probability and time discount factor damage the quality of donor pool. Using the survey data, we obtain the following results:

  1. Potential donors having high expectation are more likely to provide their own stem cells.
  2. Among them, those who discount future utility less are more likely to provide.
  3. TV commercials stimulating social desirability of provision decrease the probability of provision.
